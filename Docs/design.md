Design Document: Home Furniture E-Commerce Website
1. Project Overview
The Home Furniture Website is a modern e-commerce platform designed to provide a seamless and inspiring shopping experience for homeowners. The project emphasizes a balance between high-end aesthetic elegance and functional usability, focusing on handcrafted furniture ranging from sofas to dining sets.

2. Design Vision & Goals
Immersive Experience: Use high-quality, large-scale imagery to showcase product details and textures.

Intuitive Navigation: Simplify the journey from discovery to purchase through a clean layout and smart filtering.

Minimalist Sophistication: Maintain a "less is more" approach to highlight products without visual clutter.

Responsive Performance: Ensure a smooth, consistent experience across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

3. Visual Identity
3.1 Color Palette
The color scheme is grounded in sophisticated earth tones and neutrals to reflect warmth, quality, and timelessness.

Rich Charcoal (#050404): Used for primary text and high-contrast UI elements.

Soft Parchment (#E6E2E1): Primary background color for a clean, premium feel.

Muted Clay (#C3C0BE): Secondary background and borders.

Deep Umber (#5F5B57): Subtle accents and secondary text.

Burnt Sienna (#593B24 / #9F623B): Warm highlights and potential call-to-action (CTA) accents.

Sandy Beige (#9B8A71): Tertiary elements and hover states.

3.2 Typography
Headings: Likely a sophisticated Serif font (e.g., Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond) to convey luxury and craftsmanship.

Body Text: A clean, highly legible Sans-Serif (e.g., Inter or Montserrat) for navigation and product descriptions.

4. User Interface (UI) Components
Hero Section: Features a bold, high-resolution image of a flagship product (e.g., a modern sofa) with a clear, minimalist headline and a "Shop Now" primary CTA.

Smart Filters: Sidebar or top-bar navigation allowing users to filter by:

Room (Living Room, Bedroom, Dining).

Material (Wood, Fabric, Metal).

Design Style (Minimalist, Mid-century Modern, Industrial).

Product Cards: Clean grids with subtle shadows or borders, focusing on the product image, name, and price. Hover effects likely reveal secondary images or "Add to Cart" shortcuts.

Interactive Catalogue: A visually driven "Browse by Room" section using lifestyle photography to provide context for furniture placement.

5. User Experience (UX) Features
Seamless Discovery: A non-intrusive search bar and clear category hierarchy.

Storytelling: Content blocks that explain the craftsmanship and material quality behind the furniture.

Responsive Layout: A grid-based system that collapses gracefully for mobile viewing, ensuring that high-quality images remain the focal point.

6. Technical Considerations
Image Optimization: Necessary for maintaining fast load times given the reliance on high-resolution product photography.

Accessibility: High contrast ratios between text (#050404) and background (#E6E2E1) to meet WCAG standards.

Micro-interactions: Smooth transitions between pages and subtle animations on button hovers to enhance the premium feel
